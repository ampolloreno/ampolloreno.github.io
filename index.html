Hi! My name is Anthony Polloreno. This site will be (an evolving) home to a personal blog about my life and technical notes. You can navigate to my CV for the full time history of my career, but said briefly:

  1. I grew up in Southern California, and went to San Dieguito Academy. My grandfather frequently reminds me that my graduation quote was that "I want to learn everything there is to know." While being an obviously problematic statement at first glance, I have since discovered that even a weaker form of this goal is difficult to achieve. I'd like to relax the statement to "I want to put myself in a position to be able to understand anything I want, and additionally make an important contribution to the forefront of science." :)

  2. I got my BA from University of California, Berkeley (go bears!) Following in the wake of my high school optimism, I triple majored in computer science, mathematics, and physics. Thankfully, these three subjects are a relatively small, albeit still large in an absolute sense, subset of everything there is to know. At their intersection I found quantum computing, which has largely influenced my career trajectory since. Incidentally, after graduating from Berkeley, I was dedicated to not being a physicist, and only applied to computer science and mathematics programs.

  3. During my last summer of college I interned at Sandia National Laboratories. This is really what kick started my career in quantum computing research.

  4. I worked at Rigetti Quantum Computing in Berkeley as a software engineer and experimental physicist. My time at Rigetti was good - I met amazing people like Matt Harrigan, Steven Heidel, André Melo, Eric Peterson, Johannes Otterbach, Marcus da Silva, Nik Tezak and Will Zeng, contributed to a few papers, attended several conferences, and got a glimpse of industry life.

  5. I am now a Ph.D. student at the University of Colorado, Boulder ('sko buffs!). I am interested in quantum information.

In my free time I like to play guitar, climb, run, read, write and build relationships.
