Reservoir Computing: Dealing with Noise in Dynamic Systems

In today's pulse of machine learning, we're diving deep into reservoir computing, a subset of recurrent neural network systems that are extensively used for complex temporal processing tasks. The key question that we're wrestling with? What happens when our computational reservoir is subject to noise and errors? For the uninitiated, think of reservoir computing like a brain behind generative AI tasks - they predict future outcomes based on a history of inputs. A classic example is how chatbots predict language patterns for human-like conversation based on previous inputs.

Noise, is a natural part of machine computation. We find noise in observation data collected by sensors on self-driving cars, due to rounding in computation and even in quantum mechanics due to fundamental uncertainty. What I showed is that noise isn't just a mild irritant; it can drastically limit our computational system's capacity by exponentially reducing the collection of functions it can access and process proficiently.

In the following notebooks (and in the associated research paper), we'll unpack these findings further and shed light on the inherent challenges of noise within reservoir computing systems. We believe that the lessons learned will assist in improving computational precision and in unlocking the full potential of these systems for various ML applications!
